
Roast duck is a world-renowned dish in Beijing. It originated in the Northern and Southern Dynasties of China. Roast duck was recorded in the Book of Food Treasures and was a court food at that time. The raw material is high-quality meat-eating duck Peking duck, roasted with charcoal, red color, fat but not greasy meat, crisp outside and tender inside. Beijing Roast Duck is divided into two schools, and Beijing's most famous Roast Duck Shop is also the representative of the two schools. It is known as "the world's delicious food" because of its red color, tender meat, mellow taste, fat but not greasy characteristics.
